# Exercise and Menstrual Pain

source_id: EXERCISE-PAIN

## Cochrane review, 2019 (CD004142)

A Cochrane systematic review found that exercise performed for roughly 45–60 minutes, three or
more times per week, **regardless of intensity**, may produce a clinically significant reduction
in menstrual pain — roughly 25mm on a 100mm visual analogue scale, more than twice the minimum
change women would notice as meaningful.

## Network meta-analysis, 2024, BMC Women's Health

A 2024 network meta-analysis pooling 49 randomized controlled trials and 3,129 participants
(*BMC Women's Health*) found that all exercise interventions significantly reduced menstrual
pain. Resistance exercise and multi-component exercise programs showed statistically significant
reductions in pain intensity specifically.

## Network meta-analysis, 2024, Sports Medicine – Open

A second 2024 network meta-analysis, pooling 29 randomized controlled trials and 1,808
participants (*Sports Medicine – Open*), found that all exercise types studied were effective
at reducing menstrual pain by 8 weeks of consistent practice.

## Framing

Movement is not something to push through *despite* your period — it is evidence-backed *for*
period pain. A workout during your period is not a concession or a compromise. It is a green
light, backed by real data, not a lesser version of training.
